"The Great Gatsby" is a classic novel written by F. Scott Fitzgerald, published in 1925. Set in the summer
of 1922, the story is narrated by Nick Carraway, who becomes entangled in the lives of his enigmatic neighbor,
Jay Gatsby, and Nick's cousin, Daisy Buchanan. Gatsby is a mysterious and wealthy man known for his extravagant
parties, all in the pursuit of rekindling a past romance with Daisy. The novel explores themes of the American
Dream, decadence, and the corrupting influence of wealth. As Gatsby's obsessive love for Daisy unfolds, the
narrative delves into the complexities of illusion, social aspiration, and the disillusionment of the Jazz Age.
"The Great Gatsby" is a poignant exploration of the elusive nature of the American Dream and the moral and
social consequences of the pursuit of wealth and status.
