"1984" is a dystopian novel written by George Orwell, published in 1949. The story is set in a
totalitarian society ruled by a Party led by the enigmatic figure Big Brother. The protagonist,
Winston Smith, works for the Party rewriting historical records to fit its propaganda. Winston
becomes disillusioned with the oppressive regime and begins a forbidden affair with Julia, a
fellow Party member. As their rebellion against the Party unfolds, they face the consequences
of challenging the system's control over reality, language, and individual thought. "1984"
explores themes of censorship, surveillance, psychological manipulation, and the impact of
totalitarianism on the human spirit. Orwell's work remains a powerful and thought-provoking
critique of authoritarianism and a warning about the potential dangers of unchecked government
power.
